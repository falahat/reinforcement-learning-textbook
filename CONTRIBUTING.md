to dual-license it, or move to a different open-source or commercial license.
Outside contributions would normally make that impossible without tracking down
and getting permission from every past contributor. These terms prevent that.

In short: the project stays open under GPL-3.0 today, and the maintainer can
still relicense the whole codebase later without chasing signatures.

## Submitting

- Open a pull request. Submitting it is your agreement to the terms above.
- Optional: `git commit --signoff` to also certify provenance under the
  [Developer Certificate of Origin](https://developercertificate.org/). (The DCO
  certifies *where the code came from*; the grant in §2 is what actually
  preserves relicensing — they are different mechanisms.)

## Note

These are practical contributor terms, not legal advice. For higher-stakes
cases (corporate contributors, or if you want this airtight), a formally signed
CLA via a tool like [CLA Assistant](https://cla-assistant.io/) or a
[Project Harmony](https://www.harmonyagreements.org/) agreement is the more
robust upgrade — and an IP lawyer should review the exact wording.
